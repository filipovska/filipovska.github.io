Research

An overview of my research interests


My overall research vision is to advance the theoretical, methodological and experimental aspects of transportation systems engineering and operations research as applied to dynamic transportation networks and intelligent transportation systems. I am especially interested in developing strategies to improve the performance, mobility, and reliability of transportation systems.

To tackle the complex problems in these constantly evolving research areas, I employ a multidisciplinary approach that draws on tools from network science, operations research, control theory, traffic flow theory, machine learning and data science.

I am dedicated to working on problems of theoretical interest and importance in transportation science and engineering, and approaching them with rigorous optimization methods, as well as using data-driven, statistical approaches that make them highly relevant for real-world applications.

Dissetation Research

My dissertation titled ‘Travel time reliability in stochastic dynamic networks: modeling, path finding, and routing’, characterizes transportation networks with stochastic time-varying and correlated travel times, where reliability is an important factor for the decision making of travelers and system operators. My work proposes stochastic optimization approaches for path finding and information-adaptive routing under uncertainty and devises a framework for routing guidance to improve the system efficiency and reliability. Unifying the travel time reliability modeling and optimization problems for stochastic transportation networks and proposing solution approaches suitable for large-scale applications are two important contributions of my current research.

Recent Research Outcomes


A recent paper I presented at the 2020 IEEE International Conference on Intelligent Transportation Systems (ITSC) was awarded the third-place best presentation award.

As part of my work on a research project funded by the US Department of Transportation, I have developed a GIS-based software tool for the mapping and visualization of path travel time reliability and path finding solutions.

Prior to my dissertation work, I published two articles on the prediction of traffic flow breakdown using machine learning approaches and the mitigation of flow breakdown occurrence.
