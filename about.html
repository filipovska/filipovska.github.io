A brief overview about me


I am a PhD Candidate at the Northwestern University Transportation Center in the Transportation Systems Analysis and Planning program at the Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering. I am currently working on my dissertation supervised by Dr. Hani S. Mahmassani.

Broadly, my research is on the analysis of large-scale traffic networks and predictive analytics for intelligent transportation systems and real-time traffic management and operations. I completed my Master of Science at Northwestern University in March 2019. Prior to that I received a doal-major Bachelor of Science in Engineering (with a focus on Urban Systems) and Mathematics from New York University Abu Dhabi in 2017.

Currently, in the Fall of 2020, I am a fellow at the Institute for Pure and Applied Mathematics (IPAM) at the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA) as a Core Participant in the quarter-long program on Mathematical Challenges and Opportunities for Autonomous Vehicles.
