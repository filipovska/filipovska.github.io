Teaching Interests

Multidisciplinary evidence-based teaching approach

Like my approach to research, my teaching emphasizes a systems approach to transportation and a multidisciplinary approach to engineering as a whole. In my teaching I strive to strike a balance between providing students with the theoretical and conceptual knowledge necessary to understand transportation systems and making sure they develop the analytical skills they need to tackle complex transportation engineering problems.

Teaching Experience

During the Winter and Spring quarters in 2020, I participated in the teaching-as-research program at Northwestern’s Searle Center for the Integration of Research, Teaching and Learning (CIRTL) while co-teaching a course on data analysis for transportation and urban infrastructure systems. I incorporated a quarter-long module focusing on applications of the course material in transportation and beyond. Additionally, I created an asynchronous, virtual space for student discussions via an online discussion platform called Piazza.
Prior to this experience, I served as a teaching assistant for an introductory engineering analysis courses at Northwestern. In the Spring 2021 quarter, I will be co-teaching an engineering systems analysis course for undergraduate students.

Through my teaching experience and involvement with the Searle Center for Advancing Teaching and Learning and the Center for the Integration of Research, Teaching and Learning (CIRTL) Network, I have achieved the CIRTL Network Scholar certification in 2020.

Some Recent Projects

My presentation at the IEEE ITS Conference titled "Reliable least-time path estimation and computation in stochastic time-varying networks with spatio-temporal dependencies" received the 3rd prize for the best presentation award.
